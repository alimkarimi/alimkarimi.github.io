Machine Learning Papers, Conferences, Posters, and Workshops

In December 2024 I will attend the NeurIPS "Tackling Climate Change with ML" workshop to present my work on flood prediction. Flooding is an extreme climate event and therefore does not have a lot of data to build ML models with, especially deep learning models. In this work, I propose a method to first create a baseline of performance using statistcal models, then incorporate remote sensing to generate more validation data. The main idea of the proposal is that with more validation points, deep learning models can be leveraged more effectively.

In August 2024, I went to a Climate Change AI Summer School at Mila - Quebec AI Institue for a week where I prototyped and pitched a flood prediction solution. It was so fun to collaborate with talented early career researchers, PhD / MS students, and other professionals on various aspects of climate change! It was also nice that the program was funded for me (DeepMind and GitHub were sponsors).

In March 2024, I was able to present the current status of my research into building LSTMs to estimate Leaf Area Index for plant breeding experiments at an on campus poster session at Purdue University.

I was lucky to explore and write about many interesting topics while in graduate school. I explored various topics such as reinforcement learning for image retrieval based on human preferences, building multi-modal deep learning algorithms for crop trait estimation, and augmenting a large geospatial and remote sensing model called "Seasonal Contrast" with a inference run on a previously unseen dataset.

I am excited to keep publishing and attending interesting workshops throughout my career
