Machine Learning Papers and Posteres

In December 2024 I will attend NeurIPS to present my work on flood prediction. Flooding is an extreme climate event and therefore does not have a lot of data to build ML models with, especially deep learning models. In this work, I propose a method to first create a baseline of performance using statistcal models, then incorporate remote sensing to generate more validation data. The goal is that with more validation points, deep learning models can be leveraged.

Research Interests

I am actively researching applied solutions in climate and remote sensing. I am also generally interested in time dependent neural network architectures like LSTMs and transformers. In graduate school, I worked with Dr. Melba Crawford at the Purdue Laboratory of Remote Sensing Applications to develop many-to-many LSTMs and Transformer models to estimate Leaf Area Index from remote sensing observations.
